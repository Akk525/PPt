Methods

    Study by Noy and Zhang

  • Tasks using Generative AI (ChatGPT 3.5)
  • Two groups: Random (trained on AI) and Control (no AI)
  • Data collection through Prolific (444 participants)

    • Evaluation Process

  • Responses evaluated by three different evaluators
  • Incentives: $1 per point, additional $3 for scoring 6 or 7

    • Opportunity Cost Calculation

  • Time saved in the study assumed to be 40%

Results

    Participant Usage of ChatGPT

  • 81% of treatment group used ChatGPT for the second task

    • Performance Data (Figure 1)

  • Graph A: 40% reduction in time for treatment group
  • Graph B: Treatment group had higher mean grade (0.9 points)

    • Performance Shift in Two Groups (Figure 2)

  • Control group grades remained consistent; treatment group grades improved

    • Potential Benefits for Purdue Students

  • Time savings in ENGL 106 course: 11 hours
